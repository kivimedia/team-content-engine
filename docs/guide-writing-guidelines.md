# How to Write a Great Weekly Guide

> One doc to rule them all. These are Team Content Engine's consolidated guidelines for producing weekly lead-magnet guides that readers actually value.

---

## Core Philosophy

The guide is for the **reader**, not the operator. Zero internal content. No post schedules, no creative briefs, no fulfillment checklists. If someone paid $49 for this guide, they should feel they got their money's worth. The reader gave you their email - that's currency. Respect it.

---

## Required Structure (Mandatory Order)

### 1. Opening Narrative
- Hook with a **real** statistic, study, or event from the research brief
- Copy verified claims with their sources directly - don't paraphrase and lose attribution
- Weave in the audience's frustration naturally (don't label it)
- NO invented characters, fictional stories, or made-up companies

### 2. Callout Box
- One key insight from the opening, visually distinct
- Style: amber callout labeled "KEY INSIGHT"

### 3. Quick Win (15-Minute Exercise)
- Single concrete exercise completable in under 15 minutes
- Must produce a **visible result**: a filled worksheet, a score, a decision
- Include a table the reader physically fills in (not "think about X")
- Binary or scored outcome: "If your score is above X, you need Y. Below X means Z."
- Appears **before** the main framework - reader has already *done* something

### 4. Comparison Table
- Before/after belief shift, shown visually
- Not just "bad vs. good" - show what the reader currently believes that is **wrong**
- A genuine paradigm shift they didn't expect

### 5. Framework (3-5 Numbered Steps)
- Each step completable in **one sitting** (not "evaluate your entire org")
- At least 2 steps include a fill-in template, checklist, or scoring rubric
- Every step ends with a deliverable: "After this step you'll have: [a list / a score / a completed template / a decision]"
- Ban vague actions ("identify", "consider", "evaluate") unless paired with a specific method or tool

### 6. Scenarios (3-5 Situations)
- "What to do when..." real situations the reader has faced or will face
- Each includes a concrete recommended response with reasoning

### 7. Closing
- Bold headline summarizing the core message
- "You now have" list: 3-4 concrete things the reader produced during the guide
- Soft CTA (invitation to connect or learn more, never hard-sell)

You may add extra narrative or callout sections between framework steps for flow, but the core 7-section order is non-negotiable.

---

## Evidence Rules

These are strict. Low accuracy = instant quality-gate failure.

**Do this:**
- Every factual claim includes source name in parentheses
- Example: "Resolution rates jumped to 73% (Intercom Fin Apex benchmarks, VentureBeat March 2026)"
- At least 5 specific numbers/stats with named sources
- Include caveats when the research provides them - nuance builds trust
- Only reference companies/orgs that appear in the research brief

**Never do this:**
- "Studies show" / "Research indicates" / "Experts say" / "According to research" / "Data suggests"
- All are claims without naming the source = zero accuracy points

## Anti-Slop Rules (Banned Phrases)

| Category | Banned |
|----------|--------|
| Dead openers | "In today's..." / "In an era of..." / "In the rapidly evolving..." |
| Fake intimacy | "It's no secret..." / "The landscape is shifting..." |
| Filler transitions | "Here's the thing:" / "Let's dive in" / "Let's break it down" |
| Buzzwords | "Game-changer" / "Unlock" / "Leverage" / "Navigate" / "Harness" / "Embrace" / "Empower" / "Revolutionize" / "Cutting-edge" |
| Lazy transitions | Rhetorical questions ("But what does this mean for you?") |
| Generic filler | Any sentence that could appear in any guide on any topic |

---

## Quality Scoring (6 Dimensions)

Every guide is assessed on a 1-10 scale across six dimensions. **Composite must hit 8.0+ to ship.**

| Dimension | What 8+ Looks Like | What Fails |
|-----------|--------------------|------------|
| **Practical** | At least 2 framework steps with fill-in templates, checklists, or rubrics. Quick Win produces real output. | Vague actions like "evaluate your process" |
| **Valuable** | 3+ insights the reader can't easily Google. Saves real time/money with specifics. | Generic overview. Nothing beyond a blog post. |
| **Generous** | Complete, usable framework. Reader can implement without buying anything else. | Teaser content. "Contact us for the full method." |
| **Accurate** | Every major claim cites a specific study, company, or data point by name. | "Studies show" without naming the study. |
| **Quick Win** | 15-min exercise producing something the reader keeps (scored audit, worksheet, decision). | "You'll have more clarity" is not an output. |
| **Transformation** | Clear before/after belief shift. Reader thinks differently about the topic. | Reader just confirms what they already thought. |

**Calibration:**
- 8-10: A professional would thank you for this
- 5-7: Adequate but forgettable - covers the topic without depth
- 1-4: Generic, vague, or actively misleading

---

## Design Principles

- Write as if the reader is a **smart professional** who values their time
- Use specific numbers, names, and examples - never generic filler
- Length: **2,000-4,000 words** of actual content (not counting structure/formatting)
- Tone: conversational, like a knowledgeable friend - not corporate or robotic
- Never use emdashes or double dashes - use a single dash (-)

---

## The Generosity Test

Before finalizing, ask:

1. Does this contain at least 3 things the reader couldn't easily find via Google?
2. Could the reader implement the framework without buying anything else?
3. Are there at least 2 fill-in templates they can use today?
4. Would a professional feel this was worth $49?
5. Does the Quick Win produce something tangible in 15 minutes?

If any answer is no, add more substance before shipping.

---

## How Guides Fit the Weekly System

- One guide per week, tied to the weekly theme
- All 5 daily posts share one CTA keyword pointing to the guide
- The guide connects organically to daily post topics (not forced)
- Monday sets context, Tuesday gives tools, Wednesday challenges assumptions, Thursday proves it works, Friday zooms out - the guide ties it all together
- If the guide isn't ready, fall back to no-asset CTAs (group invite, waitlist, DM conversation) - never promise a guide that doesn't exist
